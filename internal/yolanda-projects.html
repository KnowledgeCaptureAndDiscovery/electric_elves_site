For Agents Only : Index

Related Projects

(KA)2: Building Ontologies for the Internet: A Mid Term Report" V. R. Benajsmins et al. 1999.

KA(2): Knowledge Annotation Initiative of the Knowledge Acquisition Community. A joint initiative to develop an ontology that models the knowledge acquisition community (its researchers, topics, products, etc.), as a basis to annotate WWW documents of the knowledge acquisition community in order to enable intelligent access to these documents.

http://www.aifb.uni-karlsruhe.de/WBS/broker/KA2.html.

http://ka2portal.aifb.uni-karlsruhe.de/

LARKS and A-Match: RETSINA's matchmaker.

From CMU. Available on-line.

http://www.cs.cmu.edu/~softagents/a-match/

"An experiment in the design of software agents", Kautz, Henry; Selman, Bart; Coen, Michael; Ketchpel, Steven. Proceedings of the Twelfth National Conference on Artificial Intelligence, AAAI-94, 1994, Seattle, WA.

VisitorBot (I think): They built and tested an agent system that addresses the real-world problem of handling the activities involved in scheduling a visitor to their laboratory. From AT&T Bell Labs.

"Towards an intelligent electronic secretary", Katia Sycara and Dajun Zeng. CIKM-94 (International Conference on Information and Knowledge Management), Intellignet Information Agents Workshop, Gaithersburg, MD, 1994.

Visitor-Hoster: an intelligent electronic secretary. From CMU, may have been recently renamed "visitor hosting system (VHS)".

"Yenta: A Multi-Agent, Referral Based Matchmaking System", Foner, Leonard N. First International Conference on Autonomous Agents, AA-97, Marina del Rey, CA, 1997.

Yenta is a distributed, privacy-protected, agent-based system that finds clusters of people with common interests. From MIT Media Lab.

http://agents.www.media.mit.edu/groups/agents/projects/

"Agents for Expertise Location", Vivacqua, Adriana S., 1999 AAAI Spring Symposium on Intelligent Agents in Cyberspace, Stanford, CA, 1999.

The Expert Finder helps people find experts who can assist them with their problems. In this way, the agents help leverage knowledge from a community of people. From MIT Media Lab.

http://www.media.mit.edu/~adriana/projects/EF/

"A personal learning apprentice", Dent, Lisa; Boticario, Jesus; McDermott, John; Mitchell, Tom; and Zabowski, David, In Proceedings of the Tenth National Conference on Artificial Intelligence, AAAI-92, San Jose, CA, p96-103.

This project is not so directly related but we should be aware of it: CAP, an assistant for managing a meeting calendar. From CMU (I don't think it is still ongoing).

"Coordination of Multiple Intelligent Software Agents." K. Sycara and D. Zeng. International Journal of Cooperative Information Systems, Vol. 5, No. 2 and 3, 1996.

This 30-page paper talks about their "distributed and adaptive agent architecture for problem solving", or something to that extent. the Visitor-Hoster is described as one of the actual applications of that framework.

http://www.ri.cmu.edu/pub_files/pub1/sycara_katia_1996_9/sycara_katia_1996_9.ps.gz.
